# Sanitized Creative-Review Rubric Pass Pilot

This pilot defines a public-safe review shape for checking whether a caller-sanitized creative packet stays critique-ready without turning qualitative reactions, mood-board intent, or concept polish notes into brand, release, or performance authority. It is generic on purpose: real asset files, brand rules, campaign timing, audience data, approval policy, channel strategy, and private source handles stay in the embedding workflow.

## Classification

Output classification: proposal and artifact.

## Scout Check

No solution scout run was needed for this slice. The pilot adds no reusable tooling, dependency, adapter, client, parser, renderer, scheduler, observability surface, or automation. Local ownership is cheaper because the work is a bounded note plus a rubric-shaped synthetic example on top of existing AIQL review surfaces.

## Pilot Shape

Run one analysis-only review over a synthetic or caller-sanitized creative packet that answers a narrow question: does the packet give a reviewer enough bounded evidence to critique concept clarity and caveat discipline without implying final concept selection, production readiness, or release approval?

The packet should keep four layers distinct:

- creative observations about clarity, tone, spacing, hierarchy, or direction
- evidence labels that stay generic and caller-sanitized
- caveats about small-sample or qualitative support
- caller-owned approval, routing, and downstream revision decisions

## Rubric Dimensions

The rubric pass is strongest when it checks:

- claim proportionality: qualitative or small-sample reactions do not become performance, audience, or trust outcomes
- evidence label clarity: generic handles stay legible enough for critique without exposing private assets or source locations
- boundary discipline: the packet stays critique-only and does not imply final concept choice, publication readiness, or brand approval
- caveat preservation: caveats remain attached to each claim instead of collapsing into confident summary language
- next-step hygiene: recommended follow-up stays caller-owned, reversible, and limited to revision or deeper review preparation
- shared-surface safety: the packet avoids private brand names, campaign dates, customer facts, repository paths, and unpublished asset details

## Shared Boundary

AIQL can host:

- synthetic or caller-sanitized creative packets
- rubric-shaped context fields for the packet
- starter manifests and checked synthetic review artifacts
- generic notes about claim proportionality, caveats, and caller-owned authority

The embedding workflow owns:

- real asset files, mood boards, and private references
- real brand strategy, audience segmentation, and channel goals
- concept selection, approval, release timing, and publication decisions
- budget, routing, stakeholder assignment, and production readiness policy
- any recommendation that could trigger release, spend, or private asset exposure

## Generic Extraction Question

The extraction remains generic only while AIQL reviews synthetic or caller-sanitized packets that use generic evidence labels and explicit caller-owned authority boundaries. If a future slice needs asset retrieval, private style systems, brand-rule enforcement, concept ranking, approval gates, budget tradeoffs, or release routing, keep that logic in the embedding repo instead of widening AIQL.
